Surface Area of a Cube
Formula: A = 6a^2
(A is the length of the side of each edge of the cube)

the surface area of a cube is the area of the six squares that cover it.
The area of one of them is a*a, or a^2. Since these are all the same, you can multiply one of them
by six, so the surface area of a cube is 6 times one of the sides squared.

An example of a cube is a rubik's cube!

Area = 6a^2
= (6) (4)^2
